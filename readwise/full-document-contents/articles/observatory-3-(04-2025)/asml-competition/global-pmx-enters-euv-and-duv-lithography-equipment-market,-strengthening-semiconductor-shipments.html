Global PMX's strategic focus on the semiconductor sector is paying off, as evidenced by a strong increase in shipments. In April 2025, the company saw steady revenue growth, driven primarily by significant demand for its semiconductor business. This success is largely fueled by rising orders for essential components like fluid control valves and wafer grinding wheels used in semiconductor equipment.

Growing beyond semiconductors

Additionally, the company's medical business is benefiting from restocking demand after early 2025 shortages, with products like sutures and clamps maintaining steady shipments. Combined with the semiconductor unit, these sectors contribute almost 14% to Global PMX's revenue in the first four months of 2025, an increase of 7.8% compared to 2024. This diversification aids in scaling revenue and enhancing gross margins and profitability.

Market expansion opportunities

A recent report by research firm Roots Analysis projects the global lithography equipment market to grow from US$29.26 billion in 2024 to US$65.31 billion by 2035, with a compound annual growth rate of 7.57%. This growth is fueled by the semiconductor industry's demand for advanced chips, such as those used in 5G and AI processors, which require smaller sizes and high-speed processing. As a result, the lithography equipment market is expanding, offering significant opportunities throughout related supply chains.

Strategic product development

Global PMX announced it has co-developed automation equipment sleeves and slides for EUV and DUV lithography machines with European clients. Some critical components are customer-certified and will be integrated into production starting after the Lunar New Year in 2025. These developments are expected to boost the company's growth as demand increases.

Outlook for 2025

Global PMX anticipates steady growth into the second quarter of 2025, fueled by a robust semiconductor division and the medical business's planned new mass-production projects in the third quarter. The automotive and sports divisions are poised to adapt order strategies to market inventory changes, ensuring ongoing group-wide progress.

Global PMX is committed to improving order and shipment management, enhancing precision machining, and strengthening gross margin structures to boost overall annual performance.

In April 2025, Global PMX reported consolidated revenue of NT$652 million (US$21.53 million), marking a 0.20% increase from the previous month and an 8.22% rise compared to the same period last year. This performance signifies ten consecutive months of year-over-year growth. From January to April 2025, their cumulative revenue reached NT$2.662 billion, reflecting an 18.86% increase from the same period in 2024.
