Beijing Onmicro Electronics Co. (OnMicro), a top Chinese fabless RF chip designer, filed for an IPO on the Shanghai STAR Market on March 28, 2025. The company seeks to raise around CNY2.067 billion (approx. US$281.4 million), becoming the second unprofitable firm cleared under the updated STAR Market listing rules. With backing from Xiaomi and Huawei, OnMicro aims to break the foreign stronghold on high-end RF front-end semiconductors.

From China's top research institute to fabless founding

OnMicro CEO Bruce Qian holds a master's in microelectronics from the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS) and began his engineering career at VIA Technologies. He later led R&D at RDA Microelectronics before co-founding Beijing Zhongke Hantianxia Electronic Technology in 2012. The company rebranded as OnMicro in 2019, with Qian stepping in as CEO.

Qian is part of the so-called "Unisoc alumni network," which has spawned over 30 chip startups, according to Tencent News and FX168. Notable names include ASR Microelectronics (founded by Dai Baojia), Bestechnic (Zhang Liang), and Enflame Technology (Zhao Lidong). These founders have helped accelerate China's chip self-sufficiency through homegrown innovation.

Disrupting a market long dominated by foreign giants

RF front-end chips—often called the "crown jewels" of analog semiconductors—enable wireless connectivity in smartphones and IoT hardware by managing signal reception, amplification, and switching. Yole Intelligence estimates the global RF front-end market hit US$17.7 billion in 2022 and could grow to US$24.7 billion by 2028, with a CAGR of 5.7%.

The RF front-end market grew from US$11.2 billion in 2018 to US$17.7 billion in 2022, posting a CAGR of 12.1%. Key components include power amplifiers (PAs), filters, switches/tuners, and low-noise amplifiers (LNAs). PA modules lead the segment, with a US$8.7 billion valuation in 2022, projected to reach US$12.2 billion by 2028.

Global RF front-end supply remains dominated by five players—Skyworks, Qorvo, Broadcom, Qualcomm, and Murata—who control nearly 60% of the market. Chinese firms, meanwhile, account for less than 5% of the premium 5G RF module space. East Money notes this gap as a core reason Qian launched OnMicro.

OnMicro's product lineup spans GaAs, CMOS, and SOI-based RF chips, covering 2G to 5G front-end modules, RF SoCs, IoT SoCs, MCUs, and power management ICs. Its chips are used by every top 10 smartphone brand—except Apple—and have gained traction with leading IoT and industrial electronics customers.

Revenues double in three years, while losses narrow

OnMicro has secured backing across 13 funding rounds from investors including Xiaomi Yangtze Industrial Fund, Huawei's Hubble Investment, Lenovo Capital, and Shenzhen Capital Group. Both Xiaomi and Huawei each own a 4.16% stake, making them the company's fifth- and sixth-largest shareholders.

Operating as a fabless semiconductor company, OnMicro outsources manufacturing to partners such as WIN Semiconductors, Tower Semiconductor, and JCET. The company posted revenues of CNY923 million in 2022, CNY1.695 billion in 2023, and CNY2.101 billion in 2024—a compound annual growth rate of 50.88%.

As per the 2024 Hurun Global Unicorn Index released on April 9, OnMicro is valued at CNY8.5 billion, ranking 976th worldwide. The listing reflects its emerging status within China's semiconductor sector.

IPO filings show RF front-end chips contributed CNY747 million in 2022, CNY1.47 billion in 2023, and CNY1.79 billion in 2024—representing over 85% of annual revenue by 2024. RF SoC revenue reached CNY295 million in 2024, or roughly 14% of the total.

Gross margins climbed from 17.06% in 2022 to 20.22% in 2024, though still trailing industry benchmarks. RF SoCs yield stronger margins, while front-end PAs remain margin-constrained due to pricing pressure. Net losses narrowed from CNY450 million in 2023 to CNY64.7 million in 2024, though cumulative losses have reached CNY1.239 billion—just above net assets of CNY977 million.

The company plans to use IPO proceeds to ramp up R&D for next-generation 5G RF modules and SoCs while also expanding its corporate headquarters and R&D facility in Beijing.

Corporate governance under the spotlight

As reported by 36Kr, Bruce Qian reinforced his control over OnMicro in 2020 by borrowing CNY50 million to invest through a fund under his management. The company also implemented a dual-class share structure, granting A-shares ten times the voting power of B-shares. This setup gives Qian 62.43% of total voting rights through direct and aligned interests.

During the reporting period, roughly 70% of OnMicro's revenue came from its top five customers, while over 60% of procurement was concentrated among five core suppliers. As of 2024, inventory totaled CNY920 million, including a CNY40.25 million write-down—prompting scrutiny over cash flow health and operational risk.

Domestic substitution at a critical juncture

Driven by the rollout of 5G and the expansion of IoT infrastructure, global demand for RF modules continues to accelerate, according to Yole Intelligence. OnMicro ranked third among domestic RF chip design firms in 2024—trailing only Maxscend Technologies and Vanchip—and has begun to gain visibility overseas.

That said, OnMicro acknowledges gaps in R&D depth, ecosystem alignment, and supply chain robustness compared to global peers. Whether the company can transition from niche deployment to sustainable profitability remains uncertain.

OnMicro aims to strengthen partnerships with key customers and scale up investment in advanced chip design. Whether it can emerge as a dominant player in China's semiconductor sector is a trend closely watched by investors.
