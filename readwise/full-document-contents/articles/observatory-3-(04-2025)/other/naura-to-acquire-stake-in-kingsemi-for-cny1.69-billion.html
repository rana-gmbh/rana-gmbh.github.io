Chinese Semiconductor equipment manufacturer Naura Technology recently announced plans to acquire a stake in its local counterpart, Kingsemi, for a total of nearly CNY1.69 billion (approx. US$233.2 million).

Naura's board of directors authorized the acquisition of a 9.49% stake in its local competitor, Kingsemi, on March 10. This transaction would establish Naura as a main shareholder of Kingsemi.

Set to become Kingsemi's largest shareholder

Kingsemi announced on March 5 that open market trading would be suspended effective the following day due to a change in control. Speculation regarding mergers and acquisitions commenced, and after five days, Naura was disclosed as the acquirer, leading to a significant market reaction.

This acquisition enables Naura to effectively penetrate the essential front-end photolithography process within the semiconductor sector, thereby substantially augmenting its upstream product portfolio, according to industry observers.

The transaction involved a share transfer agreement between Kingsemi's shareholder Shenyang Advanced Manufacturing Technology Industry and Naura. According to the agreement, Advanced Manufacturing will transfer its shares to Naura for a total price of CNY1.687 billion, representing 9.49% of Kingsemi's total equity.

Shenyang Zhongke Tiansheng Automation Technology, another major shareholder of Kingsemi, has also declared its intention to transfer its shares through public trading. This transfer will represent approximately 8.41% of Kingsemi's equity.

Industry observers anticipate that upon the completion of these two transactions, Naura will possess approximately 17.9% of Kingsemi's shares, surpassing the current largest shareholder, Liaoning Kefa Industrial, which holds 10.61%. This will allow Naura to achieve operational control and strengthen its leadership in the semiconductor equipment sector.

Accessing critical front-end semiconductor equipment market

Naura presently possesses a market capitalization of roughly CNY240 billion, establishing it as the most valuable semiconductor equipment firm listed on the Shanghai Stock Exchange. Before this acquisition, Naura possessed no shares in Kingsemi, signifying its assertive entry into the vital sector of front-end semiconductor equipment.

Naura indicated that the complementary characteristics of both companies' product lines are anticipated to generate synergies following the merger, in accordance with China's overarching strategic development objectives for the semiconductor sector by facilitating resource integration and bolstering competitiveness.

Historically, Kingsemi has focused on front-end semiconductor equipment, including photolithography machinery, cleaning devices, and advanced packaging and compound semiconductor tools, gaining the endorsement of China's domestic wafer manufacturers such as SMIC and Hua Hong Semiconductor.

According to Kingsemi's 2024 annual report, revenue reached CNY1.77 billion, a 3.09% increase year-over-year, while net profit was CNY211 million, a 15.85% decrease from the previous year.

Naura's main product lines include etching, thin film deposition, furnace tubes, annealing, cleaning, and crystal growth equipment, solidifying its position as a domestic equipment market leader in China. Etching and thin film deposition equipment account for roughly 22% and 21% of global semiconductor equipment capex, respectively, and are the two primary business segments in the semiconductor equipment market.

In the past, Naura has actively expanded its scope through acquisition. In 2018, it acquired Akrion Systems in the US to expand its cleaning equipment line. In 2020, it acquired RF application technology assets from Beiguang Technology, securing core technologies related to RF power and microwave applications, which helped boost the competitiveness of its main products such as etching equipment.

Notably, at the beginning of 2025, the Beijing IC Equipment Industry Investment and M&A Phase II Fund was established, with a total investment of CNY2.5 billion, of which Naura Technology's innovative investment arm contributed CNY510 million, or nearly 20.4%.

Naura's ambitions clearly extend beyond mainstream processing areas, as the company intends to actively expand its product platform into more advanced photolithography equipment. Its ongoing strategy of external acquisitions to fill product gaps and increase scale suggests that more moves are likely, gradually positioning itself as an "aircraft carrier"-level entity within China's semiconductor equipment sector.
