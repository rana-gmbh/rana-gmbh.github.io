China Resources Microelectronics (CR Micro) has appointed He Xiaolong as its new chairman in a rapid leadership reshuffle. According to a regulatory filing cited by East Money and Cinastarmarket.cn, He was elected during the company's 23rd board meeting and will serve through the remainder of the current board's term, effective immediately.

His elevation has been unusually swift—nominated as a board member on March 12, he was formally elected both as a director and chairman by March 28 during the shareholder and board meetings held on the same day.

Before joining CR Micro, He was deputy general manager of the Strategic Management Department at China Resources Group, the company's parent. Although several senior executives, including President Li Hong and CFO Wu Guoyi, were seen as likely candidates, the board ultimately selected He for the top role.

He holds a master's degree in engineering from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China's School of Micro-Electronics and Solid-State Electronics in Chengdu. He is also a certified senior engineer with a strong background in technology policy and industrial development.

His previous positions include senior roles at the Ministry of Information Industry, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, and the National Industrial Information Security Development Research Center.

On the day of his nomination, He visited Wuxi High-Tech Zone in his executive capacity and met with Party Secretary Cui Rongguo to discuss CR Micro's regional initiatives. He thanked local officials for their support and shared updates on key projects underway in Wuxi.

During the meeting, He reaffirmed CR Micro's strategy to position Wuxi as a global hub for integrated circuit manufacturing. He outlined plans to consolidate core resources, expand into future-focused industries, and adopt market-driven approaches to accelerate regional semiconductor development.

Company overview reveals diverse capabilities

CR Micro is among the few Chinese semiconductor companies with full vertical integration, covering chip design, mask manufacturing, wafer fabrication, and final-stage packaging and testing.

Its product lineup includes power semiconductors, mixed-signal integrated circuits, smart sensors, and intelligent control solutions. CR Micro operates across two main business lines: products and solutions, and manufacturing and services. The company also provides standalone mask-making services.

CR Micro currently has a market capitalization of around CNY60 billion (US$8.27 billion). Preliminary results for 2024, as reported by Cninfo, show revenue of CNY10.119 billion—a 2.2% increase from the previous year—while net profit dropped 47.55% to CNY776 million.

CR Micro attributed the profit decline to pricing pressure from expanded production capacity and ongoing inventory destocking across the industry. It also cited higher R&D spending and early-stage project costs as contributing factors.

Strategic leadership shifts signal China's semiconductor ambitions

He Xiaolong's appointment reflects a broader pattern of leadership shifts across China's semiconductor sector, underscoring the industry's strategic role amid intensifying global tech competition, according to Reuters and PC Outlet.

Hua Hong Semiconductor, China's second-largest chip foundry, recently named former Intel executive Peng Bai as president—a move seen as tapping international expertise to strengthen domestic semiconductor capabilities.

These leadership changes illustrate China's push toward semiconductor self-sufficiency and reduced reliance on foreign technologies.

With China ramping up investments in chipmaking infrastructure and engineering talent, these executive transitions are key to navigating a landscape shaped by geopolitical pressure and export controls.
