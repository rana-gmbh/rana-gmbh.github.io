Goertek, a leading Chinese supplier of precision components and smart hardware, bucked the downturn in the global consumer electronics market with a strong 2024 earnings report. Annual revenue topped CNY100 billion (approx. US$13.8 billion) for the first time, while net profit soared nearly 145% year-over-year.

However, fourth-quarter net profit fell over 70% sequentially, driven by rising costs and typical year-end seasonality, according to reports from Sina and Jwview.

Steady revenue growth, net profit soars nearly 145%

In its March 26 annual earnings release, Goertek reported full-year revenue of CNY100.95 billion in 2024, a 2.41% increase year-over-year. Net profit attributable to shareholders surged 144.93% to CNY2.665 billion, while adjusted net profit—excluding non-recurring items—jumped 178.30% to CNY2.393 billion, reflecting operational improvements rather than one-off gains.

Fourth-quarter revenue reached a record CNY31.31 billion, but net profit dropped sharply to CNY320 million—a 71.45% decline from the previous quarter, which saw earnings of CNY1.12 billion and accounted for around 42% of the company's annual profit.

The company attributed its strong performance to a rebound in end-market demand, fueled by AI adoption and emerging technologies. Key growth areas included virtual reality (VR), mixed reality (MR), and smart wearables. These trends drove momentum across Goertek's core businesses—precision components and acoustic systems—with AI-enhanced sensors contributing to higher product value and improved margins.

Solid financial structure and generous shareholder returns

By year-end 2024, Goertek's total assets rose 12.15% to CNY82.71 billion, with shareholder equity increasing 7.71% to CNY33.19 billion. The company maintained a stable debt-to-asset ratio of approximately 59.9%.

Operating cash flow declined 23.94% year-over-year to CNY6.2 billion, likely due to increased business scale, higher inventory levels, and growth in accounts receivable.

Despite weaker cash flow, Goertek upheld robust shareholder returns. The company plans to distribute a cash dividend of CNY1.50 (tax inclusive) per 10 shares. Including interim payouts and share repurchases, total shareholder returns reached approximately CNY1.527 billion in 2024. Goertek also announced a credit line of up to CNY1 billion for its subsidiary Goertek Optical Technology (Qingdao) Co. to cover operating expenses and working capital.

AI and smart hardware remain strategic priorities in the post-mobile era

Looking ahead to 2025, Goertek aims to expand investment in AI and next-gen smart devices. The company said it will track advances in 6G, the Internet of Things (IoT), and cloud computing while scaling up its presence in precision components and smart hardware ecosystems.

Goertek's portfolio includes precision acoustics, optics, microelectronics, structural parts, VR/MR/AR headsets, wireless earbuds, wearables, gaming accessories, and smart home devices, placing it at the crossroads of several long-term tech growth sectors.
