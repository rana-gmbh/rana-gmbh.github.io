On April 22, Hygon Information Technology, a top Chinese high-performance chipmaker, posted its first-quarter 2025 earnings, surpassing analyst forecasts. Driven by accelerating demand in AI and general-purpose computing, the company saw sharp increases in both revenue and profit, sustained R&D spending, and a dramatic recovery in operating cash flow. A surge in advance orders further underscored rising confidence in China's domestically developed processors.

Revenue and profit soar, cash flow rebounds sharply

ICsmart reported that Hygon generated CNY2.4 billion (approx. US$329 million) in first-quarter revenue, a 50.76% year-over-year increase. Net income attributable to shareholders climbed 75.33% to CNY506 million, while adjusted net profit excluding one-time items rose 62.63% to CNY442 million.

Basic and diluted earnings per share came in at CNY0.22, up 83.3% from a year earlier. Return on equity (ROE) for the quarter averaged 2.47%.

Wallstreetcn.com highlighted Hygon's turnaround in operating cash flow, which swung to a positive CNY2.52 billion from a negative CNY67.88 million in the same quarter last year.

Cash inflows from product and service sales totaled CNY5.54 billion, marking a 170% increase year-over-year—a sign of improved customer acquisition and payment efficiency.

R&D stays high as contract liabilities point to strong demand

As disclosed via Cninfo, Hygon invested CNY764 million in R&D during the first quarter, up 16.26% year-over-year, representing 31.82% of total revenue.

The R&D-to-revenue ratio declined by 9.44pp, primarily due to faster revenue growth rather than reduced research activity.

East Money noted that Hygon's inventory grew from CNY5.4 billion in Q4 2024 to CNY5.79 billion in the first quarter of 2025, indicating expanded manufacturing and buffer stock for increasing demand.

Prepayments rose by CNY380 million from the previous quarter to CNY1.62 billion. Accounts receivable reached CNY2.017 billion, while cash holdings remained strong at CNY7.465 billion.

Contract liabilities jumped 258% year-over-year to CNY3.24 billion, up from CNY900 million at year-end 2024, reflecting a significant increase in backlogged orders and sustained processor demand.

CPUs and DCUs power parallel growth in AI and general-purpose compute

Hygon's CPU portfolio spans the 3000, 5000, and 7000 series—fully compatible with mainstream x86 architectures and designed with advanced process nodes, optimized architectures, and full-stack ecosystem support.

The Hygon 3 remains the company's primary market offering, with the Hygon 5 in active development and slated for release based on demand and product maturity.

For data-centric workloads, Hygon is pushing forward with its 8000-series Deep Computing Units (DCUs), engineered for large-scale parallelism and compatible with CUDA-style environments.

The flagship Shensuan-3 (Deep Compute 3) is now in testing, with performance expected to double over its predecessor and rival Nvidia's A100 in AI and data-heavy scenarios. It promises higher throughput, better compatibility, and improved thermal efficiency.

Hygon's CPU-DCU dual strategy supports a complementary roadmap aimed at cloud platforms, enterprise infrastructure, and large-scale AI training.

Analyst takes: AI-fueled demand and tailwinds from localization

According to Sina via Guosheng Securities, Hygon's quarterly results exceeded expectations, driven by the following:

  1. Ongoing innovation and performance upgrades have reinforced the company's competitive edge;

  2. Increased sales and prepayments fueled stronger operating cash flow;

  3. Net profit margin rose to 29.74%, up nearly 5pp from a year ago—evidence of improved profitability.

CITIC Securities noted that the semiconductor industry is entering a recovery phase, driven by two forces: AI adoption and domestic chip substitution. They highlighted that edge-AI use cases—including robotics, voice recognition, and embedded systems—are fueling rising demand for high-performance MCUs and memory components, creating growth opportunities for companies like Hygon.

Guosheng Securities underscored mounting geopolitical headwinds in global chip trade, citing proposed 125% US tariffs and new export licensing rules impacting firms such as Nvidia.

These dynamics are opening significant market opportunities for Chinese chipmakers, as localization becomes a national priority. Revised "wafer origin" rules could sharply limit US chip access to China, enhancing the competitive position of domestic suppliers.

Outlook: growth momentum built on product, capital, and policy

Amid rising demand for both AI and general-purpose computing, Hygon is showing momentum across product pipelines, financial strength, order volume, and ongoing R&D efforts.

Hygon's first-quarter report shows total assets of CNY31.006 billion and shareholder equity of CNY20.78 billion, reflecting a strong capital position.

Analysts suggest tracking several key areas moving forward:

● Product development: Adoption rates and benchmark performance for Hygon 5 and Shensuan-3

● Customer growth: Expansion into cloud, public sector, and AI infrastructure clients

● R&D execution: Progress in technology iteration and platform ecosystem maturity

● Localization trends: Speed of policy rollout and domestic market acceptance

● Supply chain strategy: Material access and scalable production capabilities

With a strong technical foundation and market adaptability, Hygon is well-positioned to play a central role in China's drive for computing self-sufficiency. Its long-term outlook is supported by industry recovery and evolving policy support.
