Naura Technology, a leading Chinese semiconductor equipment provider, reported its fiscal year 2024 results and issued guidance for the first quarter of 2025 on April 8, highlighting sustained growth in both revenue and net income.

Robust FY24 financials and triple-year compound growth

According to data from ICsmart, Jiemian News, and National Business Daily, Naura posted total revenue of CNY29.84 billion (approx. US$4.1 billion) for fiscal year 2024, up 35.14% year-over-year. Net profit attributable to shareholders rose 44.17% to CNY5.62 billion, with basic earnings per share reaching CNY10.57.

This marks the third consecutive year of revenue and profit growth, with a three-year compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 42.53% for revenue and 54.57% for net profit.

Naura attributed the performance to major technological breakthroughs across multiple IC equipment lines, which expanded its process coverage and market share. Robust product demand also drove higher sales volumes.

As the company scaled, Naura implemented cost-reduction and efficiency initiatives that improved operational leverage and lowered cost-to-revenue ratios.

Cninfo data shows that during the first half of 2024, 92.39% of Naura's revenue came from its electronic equipment segment, followed by 7.48% from electronic components and just 0.13% from other areas.

Strong 1Q25 forecast, expansion into ion implantation market

Naura forecasts first-quarter 2025 revenue to range from CNY7.34 billion to CNY8.98 billion, representing a year-over-year increase of 23.35% to 50.91%. Net profit attributable to shareholders is projected at CNY1.42 billion to CNY1.74 billion, up 24.69% to 52.79%, while net profit excluding non-recurring items is expected to rise between 29.06% and 58.56% to CNY1.4 billion to CNY1.72 billion. Basic earnings per share are estimated between CNY2.66 and CNY3.26.

The forecasted growth is attributed to breakthroughs in next-generation IC equipment, including capacitive-coupled plasma (CCP) etchers, atomic layer deposition (ALD) systems, and advanced single-wafer cleaning machines.

These products have expanded process coverage and driven steady market share gains across mature segments. With strengths in R&D, engineering, and customer support, Naura continues to grow its market presence and benefit from economies of scale.

In late March 2025, Naura entered the ion implantation equipment sector with the launch of its Sirius MC 313 system. The move extends its front-end semiconductor equipment portfolio to all process segments except photolithography. The company estimates the domestic ion implantation market to be worth CNY16 billion.

Acquiring Kingsemi stake to consolidate domestic equipment strength

Naura is also seeking to acquire a controlling stake in Kingsemi, a domestic semiconductor equipment firm. In early March, it agreed to purchase 9.49% of Kingsemi from the Advanced Manufacturing Investment Fund for CNY1.687 billion at CNY88.48 per share.

Naura also participated in a public auction to acquire an additional 8.41% stake from Shenyang Zhongke Tiansheng Automation Technology. As the sole bidder, Naura is expected to secure the deal for CNY1.448 billion.

Once both deals are completed, Naura will hold 17.9% of Kingsemi, becoming its largest shareholder. It is also expected to raise its stake further to obtain majority control.

Naura's acquisition strategy and product expansion come amid ongoing export restrictions from the US, the Netherlands, and Japan targeting China's semiconductor sector. These moves are seen as part of China's broader push for domestic self-sufficiency in chipmaking tools.
