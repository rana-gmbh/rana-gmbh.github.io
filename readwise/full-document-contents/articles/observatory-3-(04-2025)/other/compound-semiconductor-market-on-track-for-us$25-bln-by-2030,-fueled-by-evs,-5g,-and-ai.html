The global compound semiconductor market is projected to reach US$25 billion by 2030, growing at an annual rate of nearly 13% from 2024, according to Yole Group's Activity Manager Ezgi Dogmus. This surge, fueled by demand in the automotive, telecommunications, and consumer electronics sectors, is set to outpace the broader semiconductor industry.

Even with this strong growth, compound semiconductors will remain a niche segment within the trillion-dollar semiconductor industry.

Market growth drivers

Yole Group attributes much of this growth to the rising adoption of compound semiconductors in automotive applications, particularly in electric vehicles (EVs) and advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS).

SiC power devices are gaining traction in EV powertrains, while GaN-based solutions are being integrated into DC/DC converters, onboard chargers (OBCs), and LiDAR systems. Despite a broader automotive industry slowdown, the compound semiconductor segment in this sector is expected to grow at 22% annually through 2030.

The telecom and infrastructure sector is driving demand for compound semiconductors, with AI-powered data centers and the expansion of 5G networks leading the charge. These applications rely on InP edge-emitting lasers (EELs), GaAs-based vertical cavity surface-emitting lasers (VCSELs), and power GaN devices to enhance efficiency and performance.

RF GaN power amplifiers are becoming essential for 5G base stations, with the sector expected to expand at a 9% CAGR through 2030, according to Ifeng.

The mobile and consumer electronics sector is set to grow at a 7% CAGR, driven by increasing adoption of GaN fast-charging solutions and GaAs-based photonics and RF technologies in smartphones and wireless devices.

Strategic shifts and industry developments

The compound semiconductor industry is undergoing major structural changes. Wolfspeed has exited its RF and LED businesses to concentrate on SiC, while Infineon Technologies, STMicroelectronics, and Onsemi have ramped up SiC investments, adopting vertically integrated models to reduce wafer supply vulnerabilities amid geopolitical tensions, according to ICsmart and Sina.

Interest in GaN for power electronics is rising, with Yole Group forecasting the power GaN market to exceed US$2 billion by 2029, sustaining strong annual growth, according to Compound Semiconductor.

Semiconductor giants are bolstering their GaN portfolios through acquisitions. Infineon and Renesas have acquired GaN Systems and Transphorm, respectively, to reinforce their market positions. Meanwhile, Infineon and GF are leveraging power GaN-on-Si synergies to optimize RF production using existing epitaxial equipment.

RF and Photonics Market Expansion

RF GaAs continues to lead in consumer electronics, with Skyworks Solutions, Qorvo, and Murata Manufacturing Co. dominating the market. However, geopolitical tensions are pushing Chinese OEMs to build local supply chains for RF GaAs technologies, Yole Group reports.

Once confined to defense applications, RF GaN has expanded into commercial markets, including 5G infrastructure and satellite communications, broadening its growth opportunities.

The photonics sector is on an upward trajectory, with the compound semiconductor laser market expected to hit US$5 billion by 2029. Growing demand for high-speed optical communications, driven by AI, is accelerating the adoption of silicon photonics and InP-based technologies.

Semiconductor giants like TSMC are expanding into photonics, with companies such as GF and Samsung Electronics expected to enter the space, according to Compound Semiconductor.

MicroLED displays: challenges & industry fragmentation

The microLED display industry remains fragmented, with no single company overseeing end-to-end production. Unlike vertically integrated display manufacturing, microLED development requires specialized expertise across multiple domains.

BOE and AUO are tightening control over LED supply chains, while startups and equipment makers push forward with technological innovations.

Apple's exit from the microLED sector has dampened investment, forcing many startups into financial strain and casting doubt on the industry's long-term prospects, Ifeng reports.


Source: Yole Group, February 2025

Conclusion

Compound semiconductors are gaining traction across industries, playing a crucial role in next-generation technologies. From SiC and GaN power devices to RF and photonics, these materials are redefining high-performance electronics.

Despite geopolitical and supply chain constraints, continued investments and strategic partnerships are set to drive industry growth through 2030.
