In the lead-up to China's Labor Day Golden Week, the country's AI sector is experiencing a flurry of large language model (LLM) upgrades. Baidu and Alibaba have rolled out new flagship models, while DeepSeek is reportedly preparing to launch its R2 update, intensifying competition in an already crowded domestic LLM market.

Baidu kicked off the race at its developer conference on April 25 with the debut of Ernie 4.5 Turbo and Ernie X1 Turbo. Alibaba quickly followed with its new Qwen3 series, while community leaks hint at DeepSeek's R2 launch during the holidays. The surge marks a shift in China's LLM competition—from raw scale to differentiated strategy and architectural innovation.

Baidu doubles down on platform integration and edge AI

Ernie 4.5 Turbo and Ernie X1 Turbo focus on advanced semantic comprehension and sharper logical reasoning. Baidu is further cementing its lead by embedding these models into key services—including Baidu Search, Maps, and the Xiaodu assistant—leveraging its full-stack ecosystem in the simplified Chinese market.

Ernie X1 Turbo is tailored for edge computing, aimed at IoT and mobile deployments where resources are limited. The move reflects Baidu's broader platformization strategy, laying the groundwork for broader commercial adoption across endpoints.

Alibaba bets on open-source, modular architecture with Qwen3

Alibaba Cloud unveiled its Qwen3 family on April 29, featuring two mixture-of-experts (MoE) models and six dense models spanning 0.6B to 235B parameters. A headline feature is its "hybrid reasoning mode," which lets users toggle between fast-response and deep-thought inference paths to balance speed and accuracy.

Qwen3's open-source launch quickly gained traction, amassing over 16,000 GitHub stars within two hours. The flagship Qwen3-235B-A22B posted strong benchmark results in math, coding, and logic tasks—outpacing rivals such as OpenAI's o3-mini and DeepSeek R1.

Even the lighter Qwen3-30B-A3B outperforms larger counterparts using just one-tenth the activation parameters of QwQ-32B. It supports 119 languages and dialects and leverages Model Context Protocol (MCP) for improved multitasking and agent-level functionality.

DeepSeek R2 set to shake up the playing field

After gaining viral momentum during the Lunar New Year, DeepSeek is expected to launch its upgraded R2 model during the Golden Week. Leaks point to a Hybrid MoE 3.0 architecture with 1.2 trillion total parameters and 78 billion active ones—possibly the largest Chinese LLM to date.

Preliminary benchmarks indicate that R2 could slash per-token inference costs by over 90% versus GPT-4 Turbo, significantly enhancing its cost-efficiency and positioning it as a serious contender in scalable AI deployments.

China's top AI players are transitioning from benchmark showdowns to an ecosystem-level contest of AI agents. Alibaba is prioritizing openness and user flexibility; Baidu is integrating across its product stack; DeepSeek is zeroing in on engineering practicality and applied scenarios.

The next phase of China's LLM evolution will center on streamlined architectures, efficient training pipelines, and advanced multimodal integration. In this new arms race, agent strength—not sheer model size—will determine who comes out on top.
