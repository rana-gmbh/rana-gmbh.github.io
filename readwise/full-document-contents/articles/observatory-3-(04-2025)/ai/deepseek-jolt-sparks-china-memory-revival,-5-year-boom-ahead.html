China's AI sector regained traction in early 2025 with the breakout launch of DeepSeek, a homegrown AI model that revitalized industry confidence and reignited momentum across the memory and storage ecosystem.

As demand for AI-integrated systems—ranging from enterprise storage to servers—continues to surge, Chinese memory suppliers are anticipating a strong rebound in 2025. A sharp uptick in hardware inquiries marks a shift from pre-Lunar New Year caution to post-DeepSeek confidence, prompting tech firms to embrace open development and strategic restructuring.

From caution to confidence: Storage and AI sectors find synergy

Earlier pessimism driven by weak domestic demand and macroeconomic headwinds has given way to renewed optimism, as DeepSeek's rollout slashed AI deployment costs and opened doors for small and mid-sized enterprises. As memory capacity needs rise, suppliers now describe an "AI vitality era" reshaping China's tech landscape.

Phison Electronics Corporation CEO Khein-Seng Pua said AI is quickly shifting toward on-premise deployment, with DeepSeek's launch during the Lunar New Year already driving down system costs. Phison has teamed up with Quanxing Technology (Shenzhen) to develop a fully optimized AI training solution, now in mass production and shipping to market.

Quanxing Technology has also partnered with Inspur Cloud and DeepSeek to roll out AI appliances preloaded with the DeepSeek R1 and V3 models. Running localized inference via the 671B-parameter DeepSeek model on single-node GPUs, these systems offer a cost-efficient alternative to cloud-dependent AI solutions.

Quanxing Technology Chairwoman Sandy Huang said DeepSeek is accelerating demand for high-capacity, high-bandwidth, and low-latency storage. She noted that Quanxing's custom expansion module allows AI training systems to run the DeepSeek-R1 model with just 16 GPUs—slashing hardware costs by more than 90% versus industry standards.


Quanxing Technology Chairwoman, Sandy Huang. Credit: DIGITIMES

While the expansion card solution first launched in 2024, DeepSeek's rapid rise has accelerated adoption. Storage vendors now anticipate stronger demand and pricing momentum through 2025.

AI storage demand set to soar through 2025

A senior executive at DERA Co. (Beijing) said the company has no plans to enter the AI appliance market but sees DeepSeek as a major driver of infrastructure demand. The executive cited first-quarter growth in enterprise storage and noted ongoing efforts in cloud services, finance, and transportation. Shipments are expected to double this year, with a projected 3 to 5-year growth window for the storage industry's "golden era."

Maxio Technology Chair Xiao-ling Fang noted that during the AI 1.0 phase, large-model training and inference required extremely high computing and power resources, making large-scale deployment difficult and cost-prohibitive.

Fang said DeepSeek has shifted the dynamic, marking the start of the AI 2.0 era, where localized deployment is emerging as the new standard. She added that Maxio anticipates a breakout phase for edge devices—including AI smartphones, PCs, and smart glasses—with global AI PC penetration projected to hit 67% by 2028 and AI smartphones reaching 53%.

DeepSeek's rapid ascent has triggered a market-wide shift in China, reframing AI as a practical, scalable tool for small and mid-sized endpoint applications—rather than a purely cloud-based concept.

Still, some analysts remain wary. While DeepSeek may offer a lifeline amid sluggish demand, its sudden rise has drawn comparisons to past hype cycles like the metaverse. The critical question now is whether DeepSeek's momentum signals long-term change—or simply a short-lived surge.
