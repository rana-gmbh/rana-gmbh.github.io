Cambricon Technologies, a top Chinese AI chip designer, reported its full-year 2024 and first quarter 2025 earnings, revealing a sharp turnaround. After ending 2024 with losses, the company posted its first-ever profit in the fourth quarter and followed it with a breakout first quarter in 2025, with revenue skyrocketing to CNY1.111 billion (US$152.3 million)—a year-over-year increase of 4,230%. It marked Cambricon's second straight quarter in the black.

According to official filings and data from ICsmart and Sina, Cambricon's blowout first quarter suggests the company may have finally entered a commercial scaling phase after years of aggressive R&D spending and slow revenue realization.

Cambricon turns profitable as first quarter revenue nears 2024 full-year total

Cambricon brought in CNY1.111 billion in revenue during the first quarter of 2025, just shy of its full-year total in 2024. The company turned a profit of CNY355 million, a dramatic reversal from a CNY227 million net loss in the same quarter a year ago.

In the final quarter of 2024, Cambricon booked CNY989 million in revenue and CNY272 million in net income, marking its first profitable quarter since going public.

An analyst cited by Zhihu (via Saka Finance) noted that Cambricon had been a market darling for years, positioned as China's answer to Nvidia, even as losses piled up and shares once surged past CNY800. The recent earnings reversal suggests fundamentals may now be aligning with investor expectations.

The company credits its rebound to faster deployment of AI chips across core sectors and the launch of new product lines, which spurred broader commercial adoption and bulk orders.

Cambricon revenue jumps on AI surge, loss narrows despite cash burn

In 2024, Cambricon recorded CNY1.174 billion in revenue—a 65.56% increase year-over-year—while cutting its net loss nearly in half to CNY452 million from CNY848 million in 2023.

The surge was driven by the global AI boom, particularly the rise of large language models and generative AI, which fueled demand for compute power and lifted shipments of Cambricon's AI chips.

Still, operating cash flow dropped to negative CNY1.618 billion in 2024, down 171.7% year-over-year, mainly due to increased procurement spending, which drained CNY1.02 billion in cash from core operations.

Heavy R&D investment and soaring receivables fuel momentum

Cambricon maintained its R&D push in 2024, spending CNY1.216 billion—an 8.8% annual increase and 91.3% of total revenue—underscoring its deep commitment to innovation.

As of year-end, Cambricon had 741 R&D staff—75.61% of its total workforce—with nearly 79% holding a master's degree or higher. Average R&D compensation dipped slightly to CNY762,000.

R&D spending climbed to CNY235 million in the first quarter of 2025, up 38.33% year-over-year, as the company doubled down on upgrading its AI training and inference platforms in the era of large models.

Cambricon's balance sheet expanded sharply in early 2025. Accounts receivable rose from CNY305 million to CNY807 million in just one quarter.

Prepayments increased from CNY774 million to CNY973 million, while inventory surged 56% quarter-over-quarter to CNY2.76 billion. Cash on hand totaled CNY650 million.

According to Sina, the asset growth reflects strong demand and an active supply chain, signaling that Cambricon is now entering a critical phase of scaled deployment and market expansion.

Broad AI chip deployment spans cloud, edge, and infrastructure

Cambricon's AI chips and software platforms are now used across verticals such as internet services, telecom, and finance. The products have passed rigorous stress tests, demonstrating stability and versatility.

The company has participated in various smart infrastructure projects in China, including "vehicle-road-cloud" integration, smart highways, and AI-powered parking. In rail, it has partnered with clients on automated freight inspection and voice-enabled ticketing.

Since its 2016 founding, Cambricon has launched:

● Cambricon 1A, 1H, and 1M processors for edge and terminal applications
● MLU100 (Siyuan 100 chip), MLU270, MLU290, and MLU370 AI accelerator cards for cloud deployment
● MLU220 accelerators for edge computing

Cambricon's AI processor IP has shipped in more than 100 million smartphones and smart devices. The MLU220 alone has exceeded 1 million units in total shipments, while the MLU270 and MLU290 have received awards from the World Internet Conference and the World AI Conference.

Cambricon is also developing next-gen AI processor microarchitectures and instruction sets, alongside upgrades to its software stack to improve training, inference, model compatibility, and support for open-source and large-model ecosystems.
