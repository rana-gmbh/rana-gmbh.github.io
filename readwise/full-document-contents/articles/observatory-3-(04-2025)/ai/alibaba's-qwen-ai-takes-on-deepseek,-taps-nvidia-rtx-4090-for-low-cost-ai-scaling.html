Just six weeks after DeepSeek-R1 launched with open-source model weights and app updates, Alibaba's Qwen (Tongyi Qianwen) AI model is accelerating its efforts to compete. Selected as Apple's AI partner in China, Qwen is now positioning itself as a major challenger to DeepSeek-R1.

While DeepSeek dominates China's AI landscape, Alibaba is countering with its boldest AI push yet. In February 2025, CEO Eddie Wu announced a record-breaking CNY 380 billion investment in cloud and AI infrastructure over three years—exceeding the company's total AI spending over the past decade. This stands as China's largest-ever private AI and cloud infrastructure investment.

Alibaba pushes Qwen AI model, engineers working unsocial hours

Alibaba Cloud insiders report that engineers have been working on the Qwen inference model for months, with some pulling 2 a.m. shifts since Lunar New Year 2025. Following last week's preview release, the company has now fully open-sourced the model, accelerating its AI rollout.

The newly launched Qwen QwQ-32B boasts 32 billion parameters yet matches the performance of DeepSeek-R1's 671 billion-parameter model. More significantly, Alibaba has slashed deployment costs to consumer GPU levels, and users can now test QwQ-32B for free via the Qwen app.

Qwen QwQ-32B: smaller model, comparable performance

While QwQ-32B is a mid-sized model, it delivers large-model performance with fewer parameters, challenging the conventional belief that bigger parameter counts define AI power.

Alibaba highlights that QwQ-32B supports local deployment on consumer GPUs, significantly reducing the cost of AI model deployment.

Nvidia RTX 4090 vs. A100: Alibaba slashes AI deployment costs

Deploying DeepSeek-R1's high-performance version requires at least eight Nvidia A100 GPUs, according to Dao-ru Liu, CEO of Beijing-based AI firm Galaxy-Future.

By contrast, Alibaba's QwQ-32B runs on a single enhanced Nvidia RTX 4090, cutting deployment costs to just 10% of DeepSeek-R1's. This major cost reduction makes Qwen AI models far more accessible for real-world adoption.

Qwen outpaces Meta's Llama in AI open-source community

Alibaba insiders report that Qwen AI models have now exceeded 100,000 derivative models worldwide, surpassing Meta's Llama series to become the largest open-source AI ecosystem globally.

Industry experts believe that while open-sourcing benefits cloud service providers (CSPs) like Alibaba Cloud, the computing power barrier remains high. Whether for AI inference or training, large-scale GPU computing remains essential. Alibaba's aggressive push toward open-source AI aligns with its broader ambitions to dominate the AI race.

Qwen AI model faces market reality check

Despite Alibaba's QwQ-32B breakthrough, industry experts caution that real-world adoption remains uncertain. While DeepSeek dominates in creative and literary AI, QwQ-32B's strengths lie in coding and mathematical problem-solving. Closing the gap between the two models from a user experience standpoint won't be easy.

Alibaba takes a different AI path: Dense vs. MoE

Unlike DeepSeek's Mixture of Experts (MoE) architecture, Alibaba's QwQ-32B employs a dense model approach, according to company insiders.

The Qwen AI team also conducted two rounds of large-scale reinforcement learning targeting math, programming, and general AI capabilities. This training on 32-billion parameters has delivered notable inference performance gains.

With DeepSeek sparking a wildfire across China's AI landscape, Alibaba is now positioning itself to capitalize on the next wave of AI innovation.
