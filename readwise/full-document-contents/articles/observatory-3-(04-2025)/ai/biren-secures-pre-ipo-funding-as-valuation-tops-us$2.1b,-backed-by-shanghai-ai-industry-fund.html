On March 11, Shanghai Guotou Pioneer Fund announced via its official WeChat account that its AI industry fund led a fresh investment round in Biren Technology (Biren), one of China's top GPU startups. Several leading institutional investors and industrial capital firms participated. This marks the fund's first-ever direct investment, signaling Shanghai's push toward a domestically controlled AI computing ecosystem.

Established in July 2024 with CNY89 billion (approx. US$12.3 billion), the Shanghai AI Industry Fund has earmarked CNY22.5 billion for key AI verticals, including smart chips, AI software, autonomous driving, and robotics. The investment in Biren strengthens financial backing and fosters collaboration in R&D, industry integration, and AI ecosystem expansion, reinforcing China's drive for self-reliance in AI computing.

While the funding amount remains undisclosed, ICsmart estimates the latest round to be worth several billion yuan, likely Biren's final pre-IPO raise.

Biren: pioneering China's high-performance GPU sector

Founded in September 2019, Biren focuses on high-performance computing architectures and integrated hardware-software solutions. According to Sina and Caixin, its first general-purpose GPU, the BR100, debuted in August 2022, setting a global computing record.

BR100 boasts 2048 TOPS (INT8), 1024 TFLOPS (BF16), and 256 TFLOPS (FP32), delivering three times the compute power of global competitors. It is China's first GPU to feature Chiplet architecture, PCIe 5.0, and CXL interconnects, marking a breakthrough in China's high-performance GPU sector.

In October 2023, the US government blacklisted Biren and 12 other Chinese firms, restricting access to advanced AI chips and semiconductor tools. Despite these hurdles, Biren has ramped up R&D and market expansion efforts.

At GACS 2024, Biren introduced its Heterogeneous GPU Collaborative Training (HGCT) solution, achieving 98% inter-chip communication efficiency and 90-95% end-to-end training efficiency, resolving key multi-GPU training bottlenecks.

Biren's GPU portfolio expands with mass production

Biren has entered full-scale mass production, unveiling four flagship GPU products:

● BR106M (400W Module)

● BR106B (300W Accelerator Card)

● BR106C (150W Accelerator Card)

● BR110E (66W Inference Accelerator Card)

Biren also launched BIRENSUPA, a custom AI software platform for optimizing training and inference workloads.

In March 2025, Biren rolled out its next-gen all-in-one (AIO) computing system, powered by BR106 GPUs, fully supporting Alibaba's QWQ-32B model inference and optimizing DeepSeek-V3 on domestic GPU hardware.

Market expansion and strong investment backing

Biren serves major players in telecommunications and AI, including China Mobile, China Telecom, SenseTime, the State Grid Smart Grid Research Institute, and the Shanghai AI Laboratory.

Biren has emerged as China's most well-funded GPU startup, securing multiple investment rounds:

● June 2020: Series A – CNY1.1 billion

● August 2020: Pre-Series B – Total raised capital hit CNY2 billion

● March 2021: Series B – Exceeding CNY4.7 billion

● January 2023: Series C – Amount undisclosed

● July 2023: Series C+ – Amount undisclosed

● December 2023: Yicai reported a CNY2 billion investment from a Guangzhou government-backed fund, though this remains unverified.

Biren's backers include Qiming Venture Partners, IDG Capital, Walden International, Ping An Group, Hillhouse Investment, GREE Ventures, Songhe Capital, Cloud Bay Capital, Shanghai Guosheng Assets, and China Merchants Capital.

As of 2024, the Hurun Unicorn Index values Biren at CNY15.5 billion.

IPO roadmap: dual listing in mainland China & Hong Kong?

Biren's IPO strategy has gained traction, with key milestones including:

● Early 2024: Completed corporate restructuring and formed a board of directors.

● September 2024: Filed IPO registration with the Shanghai Securities Regulatory Bureau for a STAR Market listing (Lead underwriter: Guotai Junan Securities).

● February 2025: Reports suggest Biren is weighing a Hong Kong IPO to raise ~$300 million, though official confirmation is pending.

Chairman Wen Zhang underscores that general-purpose AI chips demand significant capital investment and elite talent. He predicts that China will bridge the AI chip technology gap with global leaders within five years.

Biren's path to global GPU competitiveness

As China's leading GPU challenger, Biren continues to gain momentum through cutting-edge innovation and strong financial backing. With Shanghai's AI Industry Fund behind it, the company is cementing its role as a key player in China's AI computing ecosystem.

With an IPO on the horizon, Biren is drawing increased global investor interest as it seeks to establish a competitive foothold in the GPU market.
