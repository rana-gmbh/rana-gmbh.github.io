Tencent revealed strong 2024 results, alongside a strategic AI focus. The company is pursuing a measured approach, prioritizing practical applications in ads, games, and cloud services, contrasting with competitors' more aggressive AI pushes.

Tencent Holdings released its fourth-quarter and full-year 2024 financial results, showcasing significant growth in key metrics. The company reported fourth-quarter revenue of CNY172.45 billion (US$23.85 billion), marking an 11% year-over-year increase. Operating profit for the quarter reached CNY51.48 billion, a 24% year-over-year rise, while net income surged to CNY51.3 billion, representing a 90% year-over-year jump.

For 2024, Tencent's revenue and net income reached CNY660.25 billion and CNY194.07 billion, respectively. Operating profit for the year was CNY208.10 billion, a 30% year-over-year rise.

Revenue from the Fintech and Business Services segment grew 3.2% year-over-year to CNY56.1 billion in the fourth quarter. The Value-Added Services (VAS) business saw a 14% year-over-year increase, reaching CNY79.0 billion. Within the VAS segment, domestic games revenue was CNY33.2 billion, and international games revenue was CNY16.0 billion. Social network revenue amounted to CNY29.8 billion.

Tencent's Weixin and WeChat monthly active users (MAUs) reached 1.39 billion, a 3.1% year-over-year increase. QQ smart device MAUs declined by 5.4% year-over-year, reaching 524 million. Fee-based VAS subscriptions grew 7.4% year-over-year, totaling 262 million.

Cautious investment in AI

Bloomberg noted that Tencent has adopted a more cautious strategy towards AI compared to Alibaba Group, which recently committed fully to AI development. Founder Pony Ma emphasized Tencent's careful consideration in utilizing both in-house and open-sourced models, similar to its approach of publishing both proprietary and third-party games.

In a presentation, Tencent noted that its capex for 2022, 2023, and 2024 were US$2.6 billion, US$3.4 billion, and US$10.7 billion, representing 3%, 4%, and 12% of the company's revenue, respectively. The firm also indicated that it anticipates capex for 2025 will be in the low teens as a percentage of revenue, though it did not provide specific figures.

According to Tencent, the investment will encompass the development of ad tech, content recommendation systems, gaming solutions, and the training of foundational AI models. Additionally, the company aims to provide inference services for innovative AI applications, including those for popular platforms like Yuanbao and Weixin AI. The strategy extends to offering cloud services to external customers, addressing a growing demand in the marketplace.

Strategic focus on immediate returns

Tencent president Martin Lau informed analysts during a post-results briefing that AI is currently in a very early stage, making it quite challenging to discuss what the eventual state might resemble. However, he expressed confidence that each of Tencent's consumer-facing applications would continue to evolve into increasingly useful and powerful products for users.

Bloomberg Intelligence released a note saying that Tencent maintains an unwavering focus on leveraging AI internally, particularly in advertisements and video games, which has a higher chance of generating immediate payback than its peers. In its fourth-quarter outlook statement, Tencent did not adopt the Big Bang approach to AI, as observed by Alibaba, which concentrates more on cloud computing and exhibits less certainty regarding long-term opportunities in artificial general intelligence (AGI). The company confirmed that it had reorganized its AI teams to enhance its focus.
