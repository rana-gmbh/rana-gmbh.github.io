Since emerging in mid-January 2025, DeepSeek has rapidly reshaped the AI landscape. As its presence grows into a second month, OpenAI has escalated its opposition—first threatening legal action, now lobbying the Trump administration for a ban. Meanwhile, in China, DeepSeek's adoption is accelerating at an unprecedented pace, fueling an AI deployment boom across industries.

Following the Lunar New Year holiday, China's government, finance, education, and healthcare sectors raced to integrate DeepSeek, driving an unprecedented surge in AI inference hardware demand. Many companies began with single or dual-unit DeepSeek all-in-ones (AIOs) for testing, but this trial phase quickly transformed the market—pushing AIO AI appliance demand to nearly 100 times its typical volume.

Within just 30 days, businesses outgrew the initial trial phase. Companies rapidly scaled from standalone DeepSeek AIOs to large-scale expert parallel (EP) inference clusters, prioritizing high concurrency and low latency. This shift has sparked industry-wide discussions on expanding computational capacity.

Huawei Ascend emerges as top choice for large-scale inference

Huawei's Ascend-powered large-scale cross-node EP framework has become the go-to solution for enterprises scaling their AI infrastructure. Its deep compatibility with DeepSeek models and AI processing methodologies has positioned it as a top choice for inference-heavy workloads.

DeepSeek's reliance on Mixture of Experts (MoE) technology is a key driver of the AI inference boom. By leveraging large-scale expert parallelism and super-cluster interconnectivity, MoE minimizes dependency on high-performance single GPUs, making large-scale AI deployment more efficient.

Chinese tech giants ramp up AI AIO deployments

Since February 2025, Chinese vendors have accelerated the rollout of DeepSeek-integrated AI AIOs, streamlining enterprise AI deployment. With DeepSeek's large-scale AI models seeing rapid adoption, manufacturers are delivering AI inference solutions preloaded with hardware, models, and optimized frameworks for seamless plug-and-play deployment.

Leading Chinese tech firms have ramped up AI AIO innovation to accommodate DeepSeek's growing adoption:

● Inspur Hairuo AI AIO: Optimized for 671B-parameter model inference.

● H3C UniCube: Enables single-machine 671B model inference and unified training-inference capabilities.

● Huawei DS-version FusionCube A3000 Ultra: Deploys full-scale 671B AI models with high efficiency.

● Baidu Qianfan AIO: Leverages Kunlunxin Gen-3 AI accelerators for multi-GPU, single-machine inference.

China's AI infrastructure expansion is now a multi-sector effort, spanning cloud computing, AI computing, and data centers. Enterprises are either developing proprietary hardware or collaborating with partners to scale DeepSeek-compatible AI AIOs. This growing synergy between hardware and software is also strengthening the market position of domestic AI chipmakers.

AI AIOs fuel rise of China's domestic AI chip sector

AI AIOs are transforming resource allocation in China's AI ecosystem, driving improvements in performance and energy efficiency. Key advancements include:

● Huawei FusionCube A3000: Implements a five-stage production pipeline to maximize cluster efficiency.

● Baidu Qianfan AIO: Incorporates security intervention mechanisms to maintain content integrity and compliance.

● Domestic AI Chips: Platforms like Huawei Ascend, Hygon, MetaX, and Kunlunxin are now core computing solutions for DeepSeek AI AIOs.

More than 80 Chinese enterprises have already deployed DeepSeek models using Huawei's Ascend platform. The expansion of local AI chip development and specialized support services has played a crucial role in fine-tuning DeepSeek deployments. In large-scale data centers housing tens of thousands of GPUs, Huawei's Ascend chips—integrated with the MindSpore toolchain—have slashed distributed training costs by up to 70%.

DeepSeek reshapes enterprise AI strategies

China's AI sector has seen a rapid expansion of DeepSeek-compatible infrastructure in the past month, fueling a surge in demand for inference computing power. Companies are moving beyond pilot projects and embracing full-scale AI deployment.

Initially, many enterprises tested DeepSeek's general AI capabilities before integrating it into their core systems. As adoption accelerated, concurrent workloads skyrocketed, making traditional linear scaling—adding AIOs incrementally—ineffective for sustaining growing demand.

DeepSeek is propelling China's AI industry into a new phase of deployment. As adoption accelerates, enterprises are raising the bar for inference performance, requiring cost-effective, stable, and low-latency solutions for high-concurrency workloads.

AI inference clusters are scaling at an unprecedented rate. What once required just a few AIOs (8-GPU or 16-GPU configurations) has now expanded into deployments with hundreds—or even thousands—of GPUs. As DeepSeek continues to fuel AI adoption, infrastructure demands are rising exponentially.

Enterprises shift from AI skepticism to full-scale integration

Enterprise perceptions of AI have shifted dramatically. Previously, LLM providers faced skepticism when pitching AI's practical benefits. Today, the landscape has flipped—businesses are actively identifying AI-driven opportunities and accelerating deployment at scale.

This shift underscores a larger trend: China is adopting DeepSeek at an unparalleled pace, with enterprises integrating AI across multiple industries.
