Global investment in front-end fab equipment is projected to increase by 2% year over year to US$110 billion in 2025, marking the sixth consecutive year of growth since 2020, according to SEMI. Expenditure is forecast to rise by 18% the following year, totaling US$130 billion.

This surge in investment will be driven not only by the demand for high-performance computing (HPC) and memory to support data center expansions but also by the growing integration of artificial intelligence (AI), which is boosting the silicon content required for edge devices, SEMI said.

Logic and micro-segment to drive industry growth

SEMI forecasts that the logic and micro-segment will be the main catalyst for the increase in fab investments. This expansion is predominantly driven by investments in advanced technologies, including the 2nm process and backside power delivery technology, anticipated to commence production by 2026. The logic and micro-segment is anticipated to experience an 11% rise in investments, reaching US$52 billion this year, followed by a 14% rise to US$59 billion in 2026.

Overall Memory segment spending is expected to grow steadily over the next two years, increasing by 2% to reach US$32 billion in 2025, with an even stronger growth forecast of 27% in 2026, according to SEMI. Investments in the DRAM segment are projected to decline by 6% year over year, totaling US$21 billion in 2025, but are anticipated to rebound with a 19% increase to US$25 billion in 2026. Conversely, NAND segment spending is expected to recover significantly, rising by 54% year over year to US$10 billion in 2025, and further increasing by 47% to US$15 billion in 2026.

China continues to lead in spending

Despite a dip from a peak of US$50 billion in 2024, China is likely to maintain its lead in worldwide semiconductor equipment spending in 2025, with forecasts of US$38 billion, a 24% year-over-year decrease, according to SEMI. Spending is expected to fall another 5% year on year to US$36 billion in 2026.

With increasing AI penetration driving higher memory use, Korean chipmakers seek to invest more in equipment for capacity expansion and advances in technology, positioning the area as the second top spender through 2026. Korean investment is expected to rise by 29% to US$21.5 billion in 2025 and 26% to US$27 billion in 2026.

Taiwan is expected to rank third in spending as chipmakers seek to strengthen their leadership in advanced technology and manufacturing capabilities. Taiwan is projected to invest US$21 billion in 2025 and US$24.5 billion in 2026 to fulfill the rising demand for AI applications in cloud services and on-edge devices.

With an anticipated expenditure of US$14 billion in 2025 and US$20 billion in 2026, the Americas region occupies the fourth position. Investments in Japan, Europe, the Middle East, and Southeast Asia are anticipated to total US$14 billion, US$9 billion, and US$4 billion in 2025, and US$11 billion, US$7 billion, and US$4 billion in 2026, respectively.

"The global semiconductor industry's investments in fab equipment have been edging up for six straight years, and spending is poised to see a strong 18% increase in 2026 as production ramps to meet booming AI-related chip demand," said Ajit Manocha, SEMI President and CEO. "This forecasted capex growth signals an urgent need for intensified workforce development initiatives throughout 2025 and 2026 to deliver skilled workers necessary for the approximately 50 new fabs expected to come online during these two years."
