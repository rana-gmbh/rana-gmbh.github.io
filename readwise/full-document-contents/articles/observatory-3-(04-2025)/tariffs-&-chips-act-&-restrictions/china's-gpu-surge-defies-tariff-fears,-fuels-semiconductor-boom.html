As the US-China tariff saga intensifies, the Trump administration's decision to delay reciprocal tariffs by 90 days offers a fleeting reprieve for the semiconductor supply chain. Despite initial expectations that tariff-related stockpiling would boost first-quarter revenues in 2025, industry sources reveal a different driver: China's voracious appetite for GPUs, spurred by its AI supply chain, has emerged as the true engine of growth.

AI demand outpaces tariff impact

Initial assumptions pinned robust first-quarter earnings among integrated circuit (IC) distributors on tariff-related stockpiling. However, WPG Holdings, a leading distributor, clarifies that the standout performance stems not from policy fears but from China's AI ecosystem.

The meteoric rise of Deepseek, a Chinese AI innovator, has triggered a GPU procurement frenzy, with momentum expected to sustain strong growth into the second quarter.

The proliferation of generative AI has catalyzed demand for server components, power supplies, PCs, and notebooks, amplifying the need for electronic parts. WPG(3702.TW) reports that policy uncertainty prompted some clients to accelerate orders, pushing March 2025 revenues to record highs.

In late 2024, WPG's acquisition of distribution rights for AMD's GPUs, significantly bolstered by support from Deepseek, led to the company exceeding expectations with its first-quarter results.

Tariff shadows loom large

Despite the AI-driven boom, tariffs cast a persistent shadow. Rumors swirl that downstream system manufacturers, bracing for potential levies, plan aggressive stockpiling within the 90-day grace period to cushion future disruptions.

However, with tariff details unresolved and export origins under scrutiny, uncertainty gnaws at the supply chain. Chinese distributors have noted that upstream suppliers have paused real-time pricing, causing instability in costs and leaving downstream companies scrambling to secure sufficient inventory.

Policy flux and strategic maneuvers

The rapid cadence of US-China policy shifts keeps the supply chain on edge. In the past fortnight, distributors have huddled with clients to devise contingency plans.

Following the tariff delay, China's Semiconductor Industry Association issued an urgent directive on April 11, 2025, mandating that IC import classifications hinge on wafer fabrication sites. WPG, however, views this as a limited concern, noting that compliance rests with original manufacturers, insulating distributors from direct fallout.

Industry sources maintain that, despite Trump's tariff volleys, the semiconductor market's core structure remains intact. China and the US sustain distinct supply chains, with domestic demand met by local solutions and overseas markets addressed through tailored strategies.

The US-China bifurcation, entrenched since Trump's first term, has primed distributors for such turbulence. Experts suggest that unless severe actions, such as prohibitions on exporting downgraded GPUs, are implemented, local demand is likely to remain unaffected.

Uncertainty tempers optimism

The semiconductor landscape, roiled by policy flux, faces short-term volatility. Distributors urge caution, emphasizing compliance with evolving trade regulations as paramount.

While first-quarter triumphs reflect AI's transformative pull, the path ahead demands vigilance as tariff outcomes crystallize. For now, the industry's focus remains on navigating regulatory currents while capitalizing on China's AI-driven ascent.

Record financial performance

WPG Holdings and WT Microelectronics, leading entities in the integrated circuit distribution sector, announced remarkable financial outcomes for the first quarter of 2025.

WPG Holdings reported consolidated revenues for March reaching NT$1,101.7 billion (US$34.23 billion), marking an increase of 51.2% from the previous month and 57% compared to the same period last year, establishing a new record high. The company's overall first-quarter figures totaled NT$2,488.3 billion, reflecting a 36.8% increase from the previous year and surpassing their forecast of NT$2,100 billion. This achieved the second-highest quarterly performance in its history.

Similarly, WT Microelectronics delivered March revenues amounting to NT$887 billion, a 10% rise from February and a 32% increase on an annual basis. Their first-quarter revenue reached NT$2,474 billion, a 28% uptick year-over-year, exceeding the expected range of NT$2,200 billion–NT$2,360 billion.

These results highlight the robustness of the sector, demonstrating resilience against global economic challenges.
