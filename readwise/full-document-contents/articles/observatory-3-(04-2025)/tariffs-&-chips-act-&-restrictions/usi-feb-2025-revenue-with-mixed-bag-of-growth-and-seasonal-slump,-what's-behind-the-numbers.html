Universal Scientific Industrial (USI), a global leader in electronics manufacturing services (EMS) and original design manufacturing (ODM), has published its February 2025 revenue report. With a strong presence in consumer electronics, automotive technology, industrial applications, and telecommunications, USI leverages technological innovation and advanced manufacturing to strengthen its position in the competitive global electronics market.

Revenue performance

Citing data from East Money and Sina Finance, USI reported February 2025 consolidated revenue of CNY3.93 billion (approx. US$550.2 million), reflecting a 4.03% year-over-year increase. However, revenue fell 17.07% month-over-month from January 2025, indicating seasonal fluctuations.

For January–February 2025, USI's total revenue stood at CNY8.66 billion, representing a 3.42% year-over-year decline compared to the same period in 2024.

Why USI matters

Leading SiP & EMS provider

As a leading manufacturer of System-in-Package (SiP) modules, USI plays a crucial role in miniaturized, high-performance electronics, giving it a competitive advantage in smartphones, wearables, and IoT devices.

Diverse industry penetration

USI operates across high-growth industries, including automotive electronics, industrial automation, networking, and cloud computing, ensuring business diversification and revenue stability.

Strong global presence

With R&D centers and manufacturing hubs across Asia, Europe, and the Americas, USI strategically positions itself closer to key customers, enhancing supply chain efficiency and cost optimization.

Part of ASE Technology Holding

As a subsidiary of ASE Technology Holding, the world's largest IC packaging and testing provider, USI benefits from semiconductor synergies and strong industry backing.

Who are USI's key business partners?

USI partners with leading global brands in consumer electronics, automotive, and cloud computing. While some partnerships remain confidential, publicly known clients include:

● Apple – Provides SiP modules for AirPods and other devices

● Google – Collaborates on hardware, possibly including Nest and Pixel

● Tesla – Supplies automotive electronic modules

● Amazon – Manufactures modules for Echo and Fire TV devices

● Cisco – Partners on networking and cloud computing

● Intel & AMD – Engages in semiconductor and edge computing

● BMW & Volkswagen – Supplies automotive electronics and smart cockpit systems

USI's deep integration with these tech leaders reinforces its role in the global supply chain.

Market insights & future outlook

Analysts link USI's February revenue decline to seasonal factors, particularly the Lunar New Year holiday and supply chain timing fluctuations. In contrast, the YoY growth reflects a steady expansion in key business segments.

USI plans to strengthen its global strategy, increase R&D investments, and expand into high-value markets while navigating industry challenges and emerging opportunities.
