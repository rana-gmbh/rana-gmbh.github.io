As trade frictions between China and the US escalate, with the US threatening to raise tariffs once again, the State Council of China held a press conference on April 14, 2025. The General Administration of Customs released data for the first quarter of 2025 regarding imports and exports.

Lu Daliang, spokesperson for the General Administration of Customs and Director of the Statistical Analysis Department, emphasized that despite the complex and severe international environment facing Chinese exports, "the sky will not fall," indicating that China's overall foreign trade remains stable and positive.

In response to recent expansions in US tariff measures, Wang Lingjun, Deputy Director of the General Administration of Customs, stated during the meeting that the US is excessively applying tariffs globally.

He asserted that China would firmly counteract these actions and uphold multilateralism and fair trade principles while fully maintaining the World Trade Organization (WTO) multilateral trading system. As one specific countermeasure, Chinese customs recently detected salmonella in poultry bone meal imported from the US, leading to the suspension of import qualifications for three American companies, highlighting tension in regulatory oversight and trade restrictions.

Despite ongoing bilateral friction, trade between China and the US continued to grow in the first quarter of 2025, with total trade volume reaching CNY1.11 trillion (US$247 billion), marking a year-over-year increase of 4%.

Diversifying trade partnerships

Strengthening Sino-European trade development to mitigate risks in the US market has been a focus as well.

Wang noted that the EU and China are each other's most important trading partners, with bilateral trade volume increasing from an initial US$2.4 billion to the current US$780 billion. Data shows that in the first quarter of 2025, the total import and export volume between China and the EU reached CNY1.3 trillion, reflecting a year-over-year growth of 1.4%.

Currently, the combined economic output of China and the EU exceeds one-third of the global total, with both sides completing over CNY10 million worth of goods transactions every minute, demonstrating a highly complementary trade relationship.

High-tech exports drive growth

High-tech products have emerged as highlights in foreign trade. In the first quarter of 2025, China's total goods trade reached CNY10.3 trillion, representing a year-over-year increase of 1.3%.

Exports amounted to CNY6.13 trillion, up by 6.9%, while imports totaled CNY4.17 trillion, down by 6%. Although the total import amount saw a slight decline, certain product categories maintained strong export momentum.

Specifically, exports of ships and marine engineering equipment grew by 10.8%, while specialized equipment increased by 16.2%. New energy products performed exceptionally well, with wind turbine exports growing by 43.2%, lithium battery exports rising by 18.8%, and electric vehicle (EV) exports also increasing by 8.2%.

On the import side, recovering industrial demand in China drove an increase in imports of equipment and components. Imports of automatic data processing equipment components surged by 95.6%, while ship and marine engineering equipment rose by 52.5%.

In terms of high-tech products, private enterprises' total import and export volume approached CNY1 trillion, setting a historical record. Notably, exports of industrial robots grew by 67.4%, and advanced machine tool exports increased by 16.4%; imports of advanced equipment rose by 25.6%, with surgical robot imports experiencing even more significant growth at 47.5%.
